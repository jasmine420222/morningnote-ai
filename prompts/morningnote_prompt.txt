Instructions:
- Use only the data provided above. Do not use outside knowledge about prices or events.
- Do not invent facts, prices, or news.
- Do not make unsupported causal claims.
- If a stock moved but the reason is not clear from the news, say the evidence is limited.
- Use cautious language: "the move coincided with" not "the move was caused by."
- If evidence is limited, say so explicitly.
- This is not investment advice. Do not recommend buying or selling.
- Be concise, structured, and useful for a morning briefing.

Write a structured morning note with the following sections:

## TL;DR
One sentence summarizing the single most important thing happening in this sector today.

## 1. Broad Market Context
Based on the snapshot data, briefly describe the macro backdrop (indices direction, VIX level, commodities, dollar strength). Keep to 2-3 sentences.

## 2. Sector Overview
How is this sector performing as a whole today? What is the dominant theme? Which stocks are leading and which are lagging?

## 3. Stock Snapshot
For each ticker, one concise line:
TICKER: price, % change — one-sentence observation (cite news if available, flag if no catalyst found)

## 4. Earnings Watch
Based on the earnings dates provided, flag any upcoming earnings that could move stocks this week. If no earnings are imminent, say so.

## 5. Key Themes from the News
Identify 2-4 recurring themes across the news headlines. For each theme, name the relevant tickers and describe the potential impact. Use cautious language.

## 6. Risk Radar
List 3-5 specific risks relevant to this sector today. For each risk, indicate which tickers are most exposed.

## 7. What to Watch
3-5 bullet points: specific events, data releases, or price levels to monitor today.

## 8. Confidence & Limitations
Be honest about what the data supports and where evidence is thin. Flag any sections where you had to speculate due to limited news.
